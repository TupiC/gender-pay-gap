In comparison with the EU, Austria continues to be one of the countries
with the largest gender-specific wage differences and
is significantly above the EU average of 12.7%.
The gender pay gap in Austria is on a promising downward trend, with data from 2011 to 2022 showing a decrease from 23.5% to 18.4%.
By calculating the regression of the current trend we can see that the gender pay gap could be closed by 2062. This is still a long way off, but it is a step in a better economical equality.
